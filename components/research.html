1. Few-shot Medical Image Segmentation

Medical image segmentation is annotation-heavy, rare lesions are data-scarce, and domain shifts across scanners and protocols are common. We study few-shot medical image segmentation: learning to segment new organs or pathologies from only a handful of labeled examples (e.g., 1–5 cases), enabling fast adaptation and robust generalization under limited supervision and real-world distribution drift.

2. AI Systems and AI Agents

Real-world workflows are messy: goals are underspecified, information is incomplete, and tasks require multiple tools and steps. We build AI systems and agents that can plan, use external tools (e.g., search, code, databases), incorporate feedback, and execute reliably—turning foundation models into practical, controllable assistants for end-to-end problem solving.

3. Medical Foundation Model

Clinical data are fragmented across imaging, EHR, labs, and notes, with strong heterogeneity, missingness, and limited labels. We build medical foundation models pretrained on large-scale multimodal health data to learn reusable representations, then adapt them to diverse clinical tasks (diagnosis, risk prediction, treatment response) with minimal supervision—aiming for robust generalization, interpretability, and safe deployment in real-world care.
